Activity 4.8.1.

Consider the functions given by \(f(x) = 5-(x-1)^2\) and \(g(x) = 4-x\text{.}\)

(a)

Use algebra to find the points where the graphs of \(f\) and \(g\) intersect.

(b)

Sketch an accurate graph of \(f\) and \(g\) on the axes provided, labeling the curves by name and the intersection points with ordered pairs.

(c)

Find and evaluate exactly an integral expression that represents the area between \(y = f(x)\) and the \(x\)-axis on the interval between the intersection points of \(f\) and \(g\text{.}\) Shade in this area on the axes provided in Figure 4.8.2

(d)

Find and evaluate exactly an integral expression that represents the area between \(y = g(x)\) and the \(x\)-axis on the interval between the intersection points of \(f\) and \(g\text{.}\) Shade in this area on the axes provided in Figure 4.8.2 in a different color or pattern than you used for the area between \(y=f(x)\) and the \(x\)-axis.

(e)

Let's denote the area between \(y = f(x)\) and the \(x\)-axis as \(A_f\) and the area between \(y = g(x)\) and the \(x\)-axis as \(A_g\text{.}\) How could we use \(A_f\) and \(A_g\) to find exact area between \(f\) and \(g\) between their intersection points?

  1. We could find \(A_f + A_g\) to find the area between the curves.

  2. We could find \(A_f - A_g\) to find the area between the curves.

  3. We could find \(A_g - A_f\) to find the area between the curves.
