Activity 3.8.3.

According to U.S. postal regulations, the girth plus the length of a parcel sent by mail may not exceed 108 inches, where by “girth” we mean the perimeter of the smallest end. What is the largest possible volume of a rectangular parcel with a square end that can be sent by mail? What are the dimensions of the package of largest volume?

(a)

Let \(x\) represent the length of one side of the square end and \(y\) the length of the longer side. Label these quantities appropriately on the image shown in Figure 3.8.4.

(b)

What is the quantity to be optimized in this problem?

  1. maximize volume (V)

  2. maximize the girth plus length (L)

  3. minimize volume (V)

  4. minimize the girth plus length (L)

(c)

Which formula below represents the quantity you want to optimize in terms of x and y?

  1. \(\displaystyle V=x^2y\)

  2. \(\displaystyle V=xy^2\)

  3. \(\displaystyle L=2x+y\)

  4. \(\displaystyle L=4x+y\)

(d)

Solve the equation you found in for one of \(x\) or \(y\) (whichever is easier). Use this to find a formula for the volume of the parcel as a function of a single variable.

(e)

Over what domain should we consider this function? Note that both \(x\) and \(y\) must be positive; how does the constraint that girth plus length is 108 inches produce intervals of possible values for \(x\) and \(y\text{?}\)

(f)

Find the absolute maximum of the volume of the parcel on the domain you just established and hence also determine the dimensions of the box of greatest volume. Justify that you've found the maximum using calculus!
