Activity 1.3.1.

Recall that in activity 2.5 we used numerical methods and table of values to find the limit of a degree 3 polynomial at a point. This was inefficient, “there’s gotta be a better way!”

Given \(f(x)=3x^2-\frac{1}{2}x+4\text{,}\) evaluate \(f(2)\) and approximate \(\lim_{x\to2}f(x)\) numerically. What can we most definitively state?

  1. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 2}f(x)=f(2)\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 2}f(x) \approx f(2)\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \lim_{x \to 2}f(x) \neq f(2)\)
