Task 1.5.18.a.

Consider a rational function \(r(x) = \frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\text{.}\) Looking at the numerator \(p(x)\) and the denominator \(q(x)\text{,}\) when does the function \(r(x) \) have limit equal to 0 as \(x \to \infty? \)

  1. When the leading terms of the numerator and denominator are equal.

  2. When the ratio of the leading terms is a constant.

  3. When the degree of the leading term of the numerator is larger than the degree of the leading term of the numerator.

  4. When the degree of the leading term of the denominator is larger than the degree of the leading term of the numerator.
