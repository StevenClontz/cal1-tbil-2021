Activity 1.5.18.

In this activity you will first conjecture the general behavior of rational functions and then test your conjectures.

(a)

Consider a rational function \(r(x) = \frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\text{.}\) Looking at the numerator \(p(x)\) and the denominator \(q(x)\text{,}\) when does the function \(r(x) \) have limit equal to 0 as \(x \to \infty? \)

  1. When the leading tems of the numberator and denominator are equal.

  2. When the ratio of the leading terms is a constant.

  3. When the degree of the leading tem of the numberator is larger than the degree of the leading term of the numerator.

  4. When the degree of the leading tem of the denominator is larger than the degree of the leading term of the numerator.

(b)

Consider a rational function \(r(x) = \frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\text{.}\) Looking at the numerator \(p(x)\) and the denominator \(q(x)\text{,}\) when does the function \(r(x) \) have limit equal to 1 as \(x \to \infty? \)

  1. When the leading tems of the numberator and denominator are equal.

  2. When the ratio of the leading terms is a constant.

  3. When the degree of the leading tem of the numberator is larger than the degree of the leading term of the numerator.

  4. When the degree of the leading tem of the denominator is larger than the degree of the leading term of the numerator.

(c)

Test your conjecures by computing the following limits.

  1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \lim_{x\to -\infty} \frac{x^3-x+83}{1} \)

  2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \lim_{x\to -\infty} \frac{1}{x^3-x+83} \)

  3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \lim_{x\to +\infty} \frac{x+3}{2-x}\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle \lim_{x\to -\infty} \frac{\pi-3x}{\pi x-3}\)

  5. (Challenge) \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\to + \infty} \frac{3e^x+2}{2e^x+3}\)

  6. (Challenge) \(\displaystyle \lim_{x\to - \infty} \frac{3e^x+2}{2e^x+3}\)
