Activity 3.1.12.

The quantity \(q\) of skateboards sold depends on the selling price \(p\) of a skateboard, so we write \(q=f(p)\text{.}\) You are given that

\begin{equation*} f(140) = 15000, \quad f'(140)= -100 \end{equation*}

(a)

What does the data above tell you about the change in demand for skateboards depending on the selling price?

(b)

The total revenue earned by the sale of skateboards \(R(p) \) is given by , \(R(p) =q(p) \cdot p \text{.}\) Explain why.

(c)

Find

\begin{equation*} \frac{dR}{dp}\Big|_{p=140} \end{equation*}

(d)

What is the sign of the quantity above? What do you think would happen to the revenue if the price was changed from $140 to $141?
